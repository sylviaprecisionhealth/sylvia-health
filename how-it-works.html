How It Works

From symptoms to a personalized recovery plan in two weeks.

Sylvia uses real-time mobile monitoring and advanced data science to build a recovery roadmap that is specific to you — not a generic protocol designed for the average patient.

1

Complete a two-week mobile assessment

Using Sylvia's smartphone app, you complete brief surveys multiple times per day over approximately two weeks. These ecological momentary assessments (EMAs) capture real-time data on your eating behaviors, thoughts, emotions, and related mental health symptoms as they happen in your daily life.

Because data is collected in the moment rather than recalled later, it reflects your actual lived experience with far greater accuracy than traditional weekly therapy check-ins or retrospective questionnaires.

What you'll track

  • Eating behaviors and patterns throughout the day
  • Thoughts and beliefs related to food, weight, and body image
  • Emotions and mood fluctuations in real time
  • Related mental health symptoms such as anxiety and depression
  • Contextual factors that may influence your symptoms
2

Your data is analyzed using precision algorithms

Once your assessment is complete, Sylvia's idiographic network modeling algorithms analyze your data. Unlike population-level approaches that apply average findings to everyone, idiographic modeling treats your data as its own unique dataset and identifies the specific relationships between your symptoms.

The algorithm maps how your symptoms connect to and influence each other, revealing which ones are most central to maintaining your eating disorder. These central symptoms are the highest-leverage targets for change — addressing them has the greatest downstream impact on your overall recovery.

What the analysis identifies

  • The most central symptoms driving your individual eating disorder
  • How your symptoms interact and reinforce each other
  • The highest-leverage targets for meaningful, lasting change
  • Patterns that may not be visible through traditional assessment
3

Receive your Personalized Recovery Roadmap

Based on your analysis results, Sylvia generates a Personalized Recovery Roadmap that prioritizes the most impactful targets for change in your specific case. Your Roadmap is paired with evidence-based exercises drawn directly from The Personalized Recovery Workbook for Eating Disorders (Levinson and Vanzhula, forthcoming).

Every exercise in your Roadmap is matched to your specific symptom profile — you receive only the interventions most relevant to your individual presentation, not a generic workbook to work through from cover to cover.

What your Roadmap includes

  • A clear, prioritized list of your highest-impact recovery targets
  • Evidence-based exercises matched to your specific symptoms
  • A structured pathway for working through your recovery
  • Guidance on when and how to apply each strategy
4

Choose your level of support

Sylvia delivers your Roadmap through a tiered support model, so you can choose the level of guidance that fits your needs and severity. Whether you prefer to work independently, with a credentialed coach, or with full clinician integration, Sylvia meets you where you are.

For clinicians, Sylvia generates a personalized treatment plan for your patient based on their assessment results, with optional psychologist consultation and full program implementation available for treatment centers and public health agencies.

Support tiers available

  • Self-Guided Recovery — your Roadmap delivered directly to you
  • Guided Coaching — credentialed coach support added to your Roadmap
  • Clinician-Integrated Care — personalized treatment plans for practitioners
  • Program Implementation — full rollout for treatment centers and agencies
The Science

Built on peer-reviewed clinical research.

Sylvia's methodology is grounded in two decades of clinical research in eating disorder treatment and precision mental health.

Ecological Momentary Assessment

EMA captures real-time data in naturalistic settings, eliminating the recall bias and inaccuracy of retrospective self-report. Research consistently shows EMA data is more accurate, more granular, and more clinically useful than weekly session-based assessment.

Idiographic Network Modeling

Rather than applying population-level averages, idiographic modeling treats each individual's data as its own unique system. This approach identifies the specific symptom relationships that maintain an individual's disorder — information that group-level statistics cannot provide.

Precision Mental Health

Precision medicine applies individualized data to optimize treatment for each patient. Applied to mental health, this means moving beyond one-size-fits-all protocols toward interventions that are matched to the specific mechanisms driving each person's condition.

Evidence-Based Interventions

All exercises in the Personalized Recovery Roadmap are drawn from The Personalized Recovery Workbook for Eating Disorders and are grounded in established, empirically-supported therapeutic approaches for eating disorder treatment.
